Intro to SVG

Links

you can find useful tutorials and information on svg from the Mozilla Foundation

Examples

A rectangle

<rect x="10" y="10" width="50" height="100">/>

note, that in SVG, y values draw form the top of the viewport down, not from the bottom of the viewport up. The same goes for 'height'

A circle

<circle cx="40" cy="50" r="30"/>

A path

<path d="M 50 50 L 150 50 L 100 150 z" fill="orange" stroke="black" stroke-width="3" />

Text

<text x="50" y="30" style="font-family:Verdana;font-size:15"> Hello, World! </text>

Group

Groups are used to contain multiple svg elements. Any transformations, or attributes that are applied to a group will also be applied to all of a group's sub-elements

<g transform="rotate(90, 150, 50) translate(100, 20) ">
<path d="M 50 50 L 150 50 L 100 150 z" fill="orange" stroke="black" stroke-width="3" />
<text x="50" y="30" style="font-family:Verdana;font-size:15"> Hello, World! </text>
</g>
